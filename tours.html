Tour Highlights

What you'll experience during your visit

Coffee Fields Walk

Stroll through our lush coffee fields while learning about organic cultivation methods, shade management, and the coffee plant lifecycle from flower to cherry.

Processing Demonstration

See how coffee cherries are transformed into green beans through our natural sun-drying process, and learn why our methods produce superior flavor.

Coffee Tasting

Develop your palate as we guide you through tasting different roast profiles and brewing methods to appreciate the nuances of specialty coffee.

Farm Life Experience

Get hands-on with activities like cherry picking (in season), composting, or planting seedlings to connect with the daily life of our farmers.

Visitor Information

Everything you need to know before your visit

Getting Here

Address: EcoFarms Meru, Nkubu, Meru County, Kenya

From Nairobi: 4-5 hour drive via Thika Road and Meru Highway

From Meru Town: 30 minute drive south to Nkubu

Detailed directions will be provided upon booking confirmation.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Hat and sunscreen
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Camera (we love photos!)
  • Rain jacket (during rainy seasons)
  • Cash for additional purchases

Important Notes

  • Children under 6 join for free but must be supervised
  • Not wheelchair accessible due to farm terrain
  • No pets allowed
  • Smoking prohibited in farm areas
  • Cancel up to 48 hours before for full refund
  • Incase of rain, tours may be rescheduled

Additional Experiences

Enhance your visit with these add-ons (available when booking or on-site):

  • Farm-to-Table Lunch: KSh 1,200 (if not included in your tour)
  • Coffee Brewing Workshop: KSh 1,500
  • Take-Home Coffee Pack: Various sizes available
  • Plant a Coffee Tree: KSh 800 (includes certificate)

Frequently Asked Questions

Our farm is beautiful year-round, but the experience varies by season:

  • Flowering Season (March-April): See the coffee plants covered in fragrant white flowers
  • Harvest Season (October-January): Most active time with cherry picking demonstrations
  • Dry Season (June-September): Ideal weather for touring and processing demonstrations

Yes! Children 6 and older typically enjoy our tours, especially the hands-on activities like cherry picking (in season) and tasting (juice for kids). We find children 10+ get the most educational value. Children under 6 are welcome at no charge but may find the 2-hour basic tour long.

We follow all Kenyan Ministry of Health guidelines:

  • Hand sanitizing stations throughout the tour
  • Outdoor activities with natural distancing
  • Equipment sanitized between groups
  • Staff fully vaccinated
  • Masks available upon request

Absolutely! We have our full range of organic coffees available for purchase at the farm, often including limited editions not available elsewhere. Premium and Private tours include coffee to take home, but all visitors can purchase additional bags. We accept cash (KES) and mobile money (M-Pesa).

We conduct tours in light rain (bring appropriate clothing), but for heavy rain that would make the experience unpleasant:

  • We'll contact you by phone if we need to reschedule
  • You can choose to reschedule or receive a full refund
  • If the rain starts during your tour, we'll move to covered areas
